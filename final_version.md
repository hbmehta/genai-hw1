# Introduction to Hemal Mehta

Hemalkumar B. Mehta, MS, PhD, is an Associate Professor of Epidemiology and a core member of the Center for Drug Safety and Effectiveness at the Johns Hopkins University Bloomberg School of Public Health. His research is driven by the mission to generate real-world evidence that can directly influence clinical practice and shape health policies. He focuses on understanding how medications impact the health outcomes of older adults and individuals in nursing homes, with particular emphasis on comparing medication safety and effectiveness, examining drug-drug interactions in cardiology, oncology, and COVID-19, and developing methodological approaches to enhance causal inference. Dr. Mehta earned his M.S. and Ph.D. in Pharmaceutical Health Outcomes and Policy from the University of Houston and holds a Bachelor's degree in Pharmacy from North Maharashtra University in India.

## Area of Focus: Drug Safety and Real-World Evidence in Older Adults

A critical challenge in pharmacoepidemiology is the safe and effective use of medications in older adults and nursing home residents — populations that are frequently underrepresented in clinical trials yet face the highest burden of polypharmacy and adverse drug events. Generating timely, reliable real-world evidence from large healthcare databases is essential to informing clinical guidelines, reducing harmful drug-drug interactions, and improving health outcomes in these vulnerable groups. Bridging the gap between observational data and causal conclusions remains a central methodological hurdle in this field.

## 3 Ways Generative AI Could Be Useful in This Area

- **Automated literature synthesis:** Generative AI can rapidly summarize and synthesize large volumes of pharmacoepidemiology literature, helping researchers identify gaps in drug safety evidence and stay current with evolving findings across cardiology, oncology, and infectious disease.
- **Natural language processing of clinical records:** Large language models can extract structured information — such as medication use, diagnoses, and adverse events — from unstructured clinical notes and electronic health records, enabling richer real-world datasets for safety and effectiveness studies.
- **Hypothesis generation and study design assistance:** Generative AI can assist researchers in formulating research questions, identifying potential confounders, and drafting analysis plans for observational studies, accelerating the pipeline from data to policy-relevant evidence.
